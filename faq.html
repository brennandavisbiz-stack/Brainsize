The test

How can 30 questions measure IQ?

Because they're not 30 random questions. Our adaptive engine selects every question based on your previous answers, so nearly every item lands right at your ability level — where it reveals the most. Fixed tests need hours because most of their questions are wasted on levels too easy or too hard for you.

Is this a real IQ test?

BrainSize is an adaptive cognitive assessment built on the same statistical framework used by modern standardized exams, scored against the standard IQ distribution (mean 100, SD 15). It's not a clinical instrument administered by a psychologist — it's a rigorous estimate you can get in 10 minutes instead of 3 hours.

What kind of questions are on it?

Visual reasoning items: pattern matrices, sequence completion, and spatial transformations. There's no vocabulary, no trivia, and no cultural knowledge required — just reasoning, which makes the test fairer across backgrounds and languages.

Can I retake it?

Yes. Our question bank holds thousands of calibrated items, so every retake is a fresh test. Unlimited retakes come with BrainSize+, so you can track your score over time. Scores naturally vary a few points between sittings — that's normal measurement uncertainty, not your brain changing overnight.

Why can't I go back to a previous question?

Because the test is adaptive: each question is chosen based on your last answer, so there's no fixed list to navigate back through. Trust your first read and keep moving — it's how the test stays short and accurate.

Scoring & profiles

What affects my score besides right answers?

Response speed and question difficulty. A fast correct answer on a hard question tells us more than a slow correct answer on an easy one, and our model weighs all of it.

Why is my score shown as a range?

Honest measurement always carries uncertainty. The free range reflects the standard error of our estimate. Your full report includes a precise point score with its confidence interval.

What is an IQ Profile?

It's the shape of your intelligence — which of the five cognitive domains lead, and how fast you reason — matched to one of eight named profiles. Two people with the same overall score can have very different profiles. See all eight profiles →

Can my profile change?

Yes. Profiles describe your current measured pattern, not a fixed identity. As you train and your domain scores shift, your profile can change too — and BrainSize+ tracks exactly that.

Privacy

Is my data private?

Your responses are used to score your test and to calibrate question difficulty in aggregate. We don't sell personal data. Full details in our privacy policy.

Do I need an account to take the test?

No. You can take the full test anonymously. We only ask for an email at purchase, so we can send your report and receipt.

Can I use my score for school or a job?

No — and be skeptical of any online test that says otherwise. BrainSize is for personal insight and training, not official, medical, educational, or employment decisions.
