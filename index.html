FAQ

Questions, answered

How can 30 questions measure IQ?

Because they're not 30 random questions. Our adaptive engine selects every question based on your previous answers, so nearly every item lands right at your ability level — where it reveals the most. Fixed tests need hours because most of their questions are wasted on levels that are too easy or too hard for you.

Is this a real IQ test?

BrainSize is an adaptive cognitive assessment built on the same statistical framework used by modern standardized exams, scored against the standard IQ distribution (mean 100, SD 15). It's not a clinical instrument administered by a psychologist — it's a rigorous estimate you can get in 10 minutes instead of 3 hours.

What affects my score besides right answers?

Response speed, question difficulty, and your pattern of answers across the test. A fast correct answer on a hard question tells us more than a slow correct answer on an easy one — and our model weighs all of it.

What does it cost?

The test is free. Seeing your full results is a one-time $3.99. If you want to keep training — brain games, unlimited retests, score tracking, and articles — BrainSize+ is an optional monthly subscription you can cancel in one click.

Can I retake it?

Yes. Our question bank holds thousands of calibrated items, so every retake is a fresh test. Unlimited retakes come with BrainSize+, so you can track your score over time.

Why can't I see my exact questions and answers afterward?

Keeping items confidential is what keeps the test accurate for everyone — once answers circulate, scores stop meaning anything. It's the same reason clinical tests never publish their questions. Your report shows your performance by domain and difficulty instead.

Is my data private?

Your responses are used to score your test and to calibrate question difficulty in aggregate. We don't sell personal data. Full details in our privacy policy.
