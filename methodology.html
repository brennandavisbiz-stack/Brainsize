01 · Adaptive testing

The test reshapes itself after every answer

BrainSize uses computerized adaptive testing, the same approach behind modern graduate and licensure exams. After each response, the engine re-estimates your ability and selects the next item to be as informative as possible at exactly your level.

That is why 30 questions is enough. Nothing is wasted on items far too easy or too hard to tell us anything about you.

Fixed-form tests need hundreds of items to reach the precision an adaptive test reaches in a few dozen.

02 · Scoring

Three signals, not just right or wrong

Your responses are scored with an item response theory (IRT) model. It weighs whether you answered correctly, the calibrated difficulty of the item, and how quickly you responded.

A fast correct answer on a hard item carries far more signal than a slow correct answer on an easy one. The model accounts for all of it, then maps the result onto the standardized IQ scale (mean 100, standard deviation 15).

03 · The five domains

Built on the structure of intelligence

Items are tagged to five cognitive domains drawn from the hierarchical (Cattell–Horn–Carroll) model that underpins modern IQ research: pattern recognition, logical reasoning, spatial reasoning, working memory, and processing speed.

The shape of your scores across these five, not just their height, is what determines your IQ Profile.

04 · Honest uncertainty

Why we report a range, not a fake-precise number

Every measurement carries uncertainty. Your free estimate is reported as a range reflecting the standard error of measurement, and your full report includes the confidence interval around your point score.

Any test that hands you a single exact number with no uncertainty is hiding how measurement actually works.

05 · The question bank

Every item earns its place

New questions are never trusted until the data says they measure cleanly. Here is the life of a question.

Step 1

Generated

AI continuously creates new visual reasoning items: matrices, sequences, spatial transforms.

Step 2

Calibrating

Served unscored alongside known items to measure real difficulty and discrimination from live responses.

Step 3

Promoted

Items that measure cleanly enter the scored bank with calibrated parameters.

Step 4

Retired

Items that drift, leak, or measure poorly are pulled. The bank stays sharp.

In plain terms

What BrainSize is, and isn't

What it is: a rigorous, adaptive estimate of general cognitive ability, built on the same statistical framework as serious standardized testing, delivered in about ten minutes and validated against the standardized score distribution on an ongoing basis.
What it isn't: a clinical IQ test administered by a licensed psychologist. It does not diagnose anything, and full-scale clinical assessments measure more domains over several hours. BrainSize is for insight and training, not medical, legal, educational, or employment decisions.

As our normative sample grows, we publish updated reliability and validity figures here. We would rather show our work than ask you to take our word for it.
