Rigorous, step-by-step reasoning

You don't skip steps, and you notice when others do. You're well suited to work where a flawed assumption is expensive, catching the gap in an argument before it becomes a problem.

Spatial and structural insight

You see how parts fit together and move in space. That makes design, engineering, visual reasoning, and anything with an underlying structure feel natural rather than effortful.

Making the most of your mind

Your profile points to clear conditions where you do your best thinking, and a few habits that turn your natural strengths into an everyday advantage.

01

Give yourself room to model

You think best when you can externalise structure, so reach for a whiteboard, a diagram, or written notes before committing to an answer. Protect blocks of uninterrupted time for deep, multi-step work; that is where you pull ahead.

02

03

Complementary teammates who make quick judgments cover the snap-decision work while you go deep. The combination is stronger than either style alone.

05 · Notes

How this was measured

BrainSize uses an adaptive assessment. Each question's difficulty adjusts to your performance, so the test spends its time precisely measuring your level instead of asking questions that are too easy or too hard. Your answers across five domains, pattern recognition, logical reasoning, spatial reasoning, working memory, and processing speed, are combined into a full-scale estimate on the standard IQ scale (average 100, standard deviation 15).

Your percentile and confidence range are derived from your performance relative to a normed model. The range reflects the normal measurement variation present in any single sitting; a longer or repeated assessment narrows it.

This report is intended for personal insight and self-understanding. It is not a clinical or diagnostic instrument, and it should not be used for medical, educational-placement, employment, or legal decisions. A professionally administered, proctored assessment is required for any such purpose.
